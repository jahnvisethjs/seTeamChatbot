# SE Team Guide

## Mission

The Social Embeddedness (SE) team advances Arizona State University’s mission by **tracking, amplifying, and strengthening community engagement and public service activities**. We bridge technology, data, and storytelling to demonstrate ASU’s role as an anchor institution and to support strategic decision-making, accreditation, and funding.

---

## Vision

To build sustainable, data-driven systems and practices that make ASU’s community partnerships visible, measurable, and impactful—while developing student professionals through hands-on, real-world experience.

---

## Values

* **Social Embeddedness**: Prioritizing mutually beneficial partnerships with communities
* **Professionalism**: Upholding high standards in communication, documentation, and conduct
* **Equity & Inclusion**: Acting with accountability and respect across all engagements
* **Learning & Growth**: Supporting continuous professional development
* **Collaboration**: Working across teams, units, and partners

---

## Team Structure & Roles



### Core Roles

* **Assistant Vice President (CN)**: Strategic oversight, approvals, evaluations
* **Supervisors / Leads**: Project guidance, onboarding, feedback

### Student Staff Responsibilities

* Support reporting, documentation, and storytelling

## Communication Norms

### Slack

* Update Slack status at the start of each workday
* React to messages that do not require a response
* Use DMs for collaboration; channels for announcements
* Same-day responses expected (not instant)

### Email

* Use **ASU email only** for work communication
* Include clear subject lines (e.g., *Action Required*, *Request for Meeting*)
* Provide context, links, and deadlines
* Be concise and professional

### Mobile

* Used only for urgent or emergency communication

## Meetings & Preparation

### Meeting Types

1. **Weekly Team Meeting**

   * 1 hour, in person
   * Add weekly updates and links to shared notes

2. **1:1 Meetings**

   * Prepare and share agenda 24 hours in advance

3. **Project / Community Partner Meetings**

   * Conduct background research
   * Prepare specific questions and materials

### Best Practices

* Always include links in agendas and notes
* Avoid vague agenda items
* Set clear deadlines for feedback

---

## Proxying into Collaboratory (Required Skill)

### What is Proxying?

Entering activities into Collaboratory on behalf of faculty or staff. All activities are reviewed and approved before publication.

### Proxying Rules

* Always assign the correct **Activity Lead**
* Every activity must include:

  * ASU Unit
  * Point of contact (not default placeholders)
  * Location (in-person or virtual)
  * All community partners and their roles
* Correctly classify **Community Engagement vs Public Service**
* Use ASU Search to research missing details

### Mandatory Training

All team members must complete Collaboratory training and regularly proxy activities when capacity allows.
